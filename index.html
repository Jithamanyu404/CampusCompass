Class & Lab Structure

  • Theory classes: 1.5 hour slots, typically 3-4 per day
  • Lab sessions: 1.5 hour continuous blocks, 2-3 per week
  • Practicals include viva-voce (oral examination) each session
  • Tutorials (extra sessions) are held for complex subjects
  • Saturday and Sunday are usually free, plan your weekends accordingly

Attendance Rules

  • Minimum 80% attendance required per subject
  • Below 80% = Marks deduction
  • Track attendance daily on the ERP portal. Don't rely on memory
  • Medical certificates can grant attendance condonation (limited)
  • Even 1–2 days of absence per week adds up quickly
  • Proxy attendance is strictly prohibited and leads to suspension

Exam Pattern

Your final grade is split equally between continuous assessment and semester exams.

50%
Mid Semester Exam
  • 2 quizzes per semester (theory)
  • Lab work and records graded continuously
  • Assignments, quizzes, and mini-projects
  • Surprise tests may be conducted anytime
  • Attendance itself contributes to total marks
50%
End Semester Exam
  • 3-hour centralized exam per subject
  • Held in November (odd sem) & April (even sem)
  • Practical exams conducted separately
  • Minimum 35/50 required to pass
  • Failing ESE means arrear

Maintaining a Good CGPA from Day 1

Each semester's GPA impacts your cumulative. Don't let early semesters drag your average down.

Stay consistent weekly

Review your notes every evening for 30 minutes of daily revision beats 10 hours of cramming before exams.

Nail every Quiz

Quiz marks are easier to score than ESE. Aim for full marks in assignments, lab records, and internal tests.

Form study groups

Group study for tough subjects like Mathematics, Physics, and Programming helps concepts stick faster.

05 Survival Kit

Survival Kit

Condensed wisdom from seniors who made every mistake so you don't have to. Read this twice.

Things Every Fresher Should Do
  • Introduce yourself to your faculty advisor in Week 1
  • Keep a physical planner or digital calendar for deadlines
  • Explore Electronics City and Bangalore in your first month
  • Start learning competitive programming (LeetCode/Codeforces) from Sem 1
  • Build one small project every semester — portfolio matters for internships
  • Attend every guest lecture and seminar — even outside your domain
  • Maintain a LinkedIn profile and update it regularly
  • Call home regularly — hostel life can feel isolating initially
  • Sleep 7–8 hours — pulling all-nighters every week backfires
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Don't skip the first week thinking "nothing important happens"
  • Don't miss lab records deadlines — they affect your CIA score directly
  • Don't spend the first month only socialising with school friends
  • Don't ignore math subjects — they appear in every technical subject
  • Don't borrow notes consistently — you'll misunderstand concepts
  • Don't keep sensitive documents (originals) in your hostel room
  • Don't wait till Year 3 to start preparing for placements
  • Don't overcommit to too many clubs in Semester 1 — depth over breadth
  • Don't ignore mental health — seek support early, not after burnout

Time Management Framework

How to divide your day across priorities without burning out.

40%

Academics

Classes, labs, self-study, and assignment preparation. Protect this block — it's non-negotiable.

25%

Clubs & Skills

Club meetings, side projects, competitive programming, and workshops that build real-world capabilities.

35%

Rest & Recovery

Sleep, meals, sport, socializing, and simply doing nothing. You cannot sustain output without recovery.

Mental Health Matters

Hostel life, academic pressure, and being away from home for the first time is genuinely hard. Homesickness, anxiety, and imposter syndrome are common — you are not alone. IIIT-B has a Student Wellness Centre with trained counselors; use it without stigma. Build a small group of trusted friends early. If everything feels overwhelming, speak to your faculty advisor — they've seen it all and are here to help.

Student Wellness Centre — Block C, Ground Floor wellness@iiitb.ac.in

07 FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Real questions from real freshers. Answered honestly.

What happens if my attendance drops below 75%?
You will be detained from writing the End Semester Exam (ESE) for that subject, which means an automatic fail. You must then reappear as an arrear candidate in the next semester, which delays your degree progress. Avoid this at all costs — missing even two weeks without justification can put you below the threshold.
Can I change my branch after admission?
Yes, branch transfers are possible after the first semester, subject to seat availability and CGPA requirements. Generally, a CGPA above 9.0 is needed for competitive transfers (e.g., to CSE). Contact the academic office for the official process and deadlines.
How competitive is it to get into a technical club?
It varies by club. Chakravyuha and CyberSec clubs have selection rounds including coding tests or aptitude questions. Design and cultural clubs are more accessible. The key is showing genuine enthusiasm and a basic understanding of the domain — you don't need to be an expert in your first year.
When should I start preparing for placements?
Start in Semester 1 by building programming fundamentals and consistency. DSA practice (LeetCode) should become habitual by Year 2. By Year 3, you should have 2–3 projects, internship experience, and a strong GitHub profile. Campus placements happen in Year 4 Semester 1 — the prep timeline is longer than most freshers expect.
Is ragging an issue at IIIT-B?
IIIT-B has a strict zero-tolerance policy on ragging, backed by AICTE regulations. There is an Anti-Ragging Committee and a 24/7 helpline. The campus culture is generally welcoming and collaborative. If you ever encounter inappropriate behaviour, report it immediately — the institution takes these complaints very seriously.
Is Wi-Fi available in hostels?
Yes, campus-wide Wi-Fi is provided including in hostel blocks. However, speeds can be inconsistent during peak hours. Many students also maintain a personal data plan as backup. Certain sites may be restricted on the campus network — a standard practice at most Indian engineering colleges.
What should I pack for hostel — mandatory and optional?
Mandatory: ID card, original certificates (keep copies with you, originals at home), personal medications, formal clothes for college (light formals or smart casuals), a basic stationery kit, and a laptop. Optional but useful: power bank, extension board, noise-cancelling earphones, a physical lock for your cupboard, and a water bottle.
How does the NPTEL certification process work?
IIIT-B encourages and often recommends NPTEL certifications for certain programs. You register on swayam.gov.in, follow weekly video lectures, submit assignments, and then appear for a proctored exam at a registered centre. Certificates are awarded based on combined assignment and exam scores. Elite certifications (above 90%) add significant weight to your resume.
Can day scholars participate in club activities?
Absolutely. All clubs are open to both hostellers and day scholars. Evening club meetings (5–7 PM) are accessible to day scholars. The key is commitment — clubs expect regular attendance for their members regardless of accommodation status.
What is the dress code at IIIT-B?
IIIT-B follows a smart-casual dress code on campus. Students typically wear full-sleeved shirts or formal trousers. Girls wear salwar kameez or formal western wear. Torn jeans, sleeveless tops, and overly casual clothing are discouraged in academic blocks. Lab sessions require formal attire and safety shoes for certain departments.
